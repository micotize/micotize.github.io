      15/01/2020     Francesco Fanelli (Université de Lyon)
      09/10/2020     Statistical solutions to the barotropic Navier-Stokes equations
      00/00/0000    
In this talk we are concerned with the notion of statistical solutions to some models of fluid mechanics, with a special focus on the barotropic Navier-Stokes equations. Statistical solutions in fluid mechanics were introduced in the pioneering works of Foias-Prodi and Vishik-Fursikov for the incompressible Navier-Stokes system. In the first part of the talk, we propose a new concept of statistical solutions for the compressible system. The leading idea is to define a statistical solution as the push-forward measure of the initial probability distribution on the space of data of the Navier-Stokes system. In order for this notion to be well-defined, we need a sort of uniqueness of trajectories emanating from a fixed initial datum: this property is guaranteed by a semiflow selection procedure, in the spirit of Krylov and Cardona-Kapitanskii. In the second part of the talk, we focus on the special class of stationary statistical solutions. In particular, we explore their role in the investigation of the validity of the so-called ergodic hypothesis in the context of the barotropic Navier-Stokes equations. This talk is based on joint works with Eduard Feireisl (Czech Academy of Sciences) and Martina Hofmanová (Universität Bielefeld).

      29/01/2020     Manuel Gnann (TU Delft)
      29/01/2020     Droplet spreading influenced by thermal noise
      00/00/0000    
We consider a stochastic fourth-order degenerate-parabolic PDE describing the spreading of viscous thin films under the influence of thermal fluctuations. This equation has been suggested in the physical literature approximately 15 years ago independently by Davidovitch, Moro and Stone, and by Grün, Mecke and Rauscher. In this talk, we demonstrate how weak (martingale) solutions can be constructed in the case of linear and nonlinear gradient noise. Key ingredients are (combined) energy and entropy estimates in conjunction with a tailor-made approximation procedure in order to control the formation of shocks.
The talk is based on joint works with Konstantinos Dareiotis (University of Leeds), Benjamin Gess (Bielefeld University and Max Planck Institute, Leipzig) and Günther Grün (University of Erlangen-Nuremberg).

      09/10/2020     Yao Yao (Georgia Tech)
      09/10/2020     Radial symmetry of stationary and uniformly-rotating solutions in 2D incompressible fluid equations
      00/00/0000    
In this talk, I will discuss some results on radial symmetry property for stationary and uniformly-rotating solutions for the 2D Euler and SQG equation, where we aim to answer the question whether every stationary/uniformly-rotating solution must be radially symmetric, if the vorticity is compactly supported. This is a joint work with Javier Gómez-Serrano, Jaemin Park and Jia Shi.

      16/10/2020     Hans Knuepfer (Universität Heidelberg)
      16/10/2020     Gamma-limit for zigzag domain walls in thin ferromagnetic films
      00/00/0000    
Charged domain walls are a type of transition layers in thin ferromagnetic films which appear due to global topological constraints. The underlying micromagnetic energy is determined by a competition between a diffuse interface energy and the long-range magnetostatic interaction. The underlying model is non-convex and vectorial. In the macroscopic limit we show that the energy Gamma--converges to a limit model where jump discontinuities of the magnetization are penalized anisotropically. In particular, we identify a supercritical regime which allows for tangential variation of the domain walls.

      23/10/2020     Martina Hofmanova (Universität Bielefeld)
      16/10/2020     Non-uniqueness in law of stochastic 3D Navier-Stokes equations
      00/00/0000    
We consider the stochastic Navier--Stokes equations in three dimensions and prove that the law of analytically weak solutions is not unique. In particular, we focus on two iconic examples of a stochastic perturbation: either an additive or a linear multiplicative noise driven by a Wiener process. In both cases, we develop a stochastic counterpart of the convex integration method introduced recently by Buckmaster and Vicol. This permits to construct probabilistically strong and analytically weak solutions defined up to a suitable stopping time. In addition, these solutions fail the corresponding energy inequality at a prescribed time with a prescribed probability. Then we introduce a general probabilistic construction used to extend the convex integration solutions beyond the stopping time and in particular to the whole time interval [0,∞). Finally, we show that their law is distinct from the law of solutions obtained by Galerkin approximation. In particular, non-uniqueness in law holds on an arbitrary time interval [0,T], T>0.

      30/10/2020     Weiren Zhao (NYU Abu Dhabi) [Different time: 10:00am]
      00/00/0000     Stability of the Couette flow for 2D Navier-Stokes Boussinesq system
      00/00/0000    
In this talk, I will talk about the stability of Couette flow for 2D Navier-Stokes Boussinesq system without thermal diffusivity for the initial perturbation in Gevrey-3-. The synergism of density mixing, vorticity mixing and velocity diffusion leads to the stability. This is a joint work with Masmoudi and Said-Houari.

      06/11/2020     Jessica Lin (McGill University)
      00/00/0000     Homogenization of an Allen-Cahn Equation in Periodic Media
      00/00/0000    
I will discuss periodic homogenization for the Allen-Cahn equation with Neumann boundary conditions. It is well-known that rescaled solutions of the (homogeneous) Allen-Cahn equation converge to generalized solutions of mean-curvature flow. Using a variational approach, I will show that under suitable hypotheses, a similar result holds true for Allen-Cahn equations with periodic reaction terms. This talk is based on joint work with Rustum Choksi, Irene Fonseca, and Raghavendra Venkatraman.

      13/11/2020     Luc Rey-Bellet (University of Massachusetts)
      00/00/0000     Variational representation and concentration inequalities for uncertainty quantification
      00/00/0000    
We use variational representation for information-theoretic divergences (such as the KL-divergences) and concentration inequalities to derive, in a systematic way, tight information inequalities. A central question in uncertainty quantification and statistical learning is how to choose the right divergences for the right quantity of interest. We concentrate on KL-divergence, f-divergences, and Renyi divergences and discuss several examples: expected values, variance and other functionals, rare events, and steady state expectation for MCMC dynamics (for example Hamiltonian Monte-Carlo).

      20/11/2020     Maria G. Westdickenberg (RWTH Aachen University)
      00/00/0000     Two approaches to optimal convergence rates for (mildly) nonconvex gradient flows
      00/00/0000    
We are interested in understanding the evolution in time of gradient flows with respect to nonconvex energies and ``poorly prepared'' initial data. We recall the algebraic in time convergence rates for convex (but not strictly convex) energies and then consider adapting the ideas for the nonconvex case. We illustrate two different frameworks with examples. On the one hand, we study the HED method for the Mullins-Sekerka evolution in two space dimensions. On the other hand, we consider an L1 framework for the Cahn Hilliard equation in one space dimension.

      27/11/2020     Cosmin Burtea (Université Paris Diderot)
      00/00/0000     Existence of weak solutions for the Navier-Stokes system for stationary anisotropic compressible flows
      00/00/0000    
When dealing with PDEs coming from fluid mechanics, bounded-energy weak solutions are, in many cases, the only type of solutions for which one can guarantee existence without imposing any restriction on the size of the initial data or forcing terms. Understanding how to construct such solutions is also important for designing stable numerical schemes. In order to construct weak-solutions, one usually considers some parabolic/elliptic regularization of the initial system for which well-posedness follows by standard functional analysis techniques and hopes that the limiting object, as the regularizing parameters tend to zero, is a solution for the initial system. In the context of compressible flows, the energy functional does not provide any regularity information for the density of the fluid such that passing to the limit in nonlinear terms, such as the pressure which is a genuine non-linear function of the density, raises some important difficulties.

In this talk we discuss the problem of constructing weak-solutions for the stationary Navier-Stokes system for a barotropic compressible fluid with an anisotropic and/or non-local viscous stress tensor. The key ingredient is a new identity that we obtain by proceeding as follows. Given a sequence of weak-solutions we take a look at the limit of the equations of the associated energies. From the limiting equation we subtract the equation of the energy associated to the system verified by the limit of the aforementioned sequence of weak-solutions. It turns out that the resulting expression can be used to recover strong convergence in L2 for the sequence of velocity gradients. A posteori we recover strong convergence for the corresponding sequence of densities. The results that will be presented are obtained in collaboration with D. Bresch.

      04/12/2020     Arianna Giunti (Imperial College London)
      00/00/0000     Homogenization in randomly punctured domains
      00/00/0000    
In this talk we discuss some results concerning the homogenization of solutions to a Stokes system in a domain having many small random holes. The main application for these models arises from the study of the flow of a viscous incompressible fluid through a porous medium or in the quasi-static regime of sedimenting particles. Our main focus is the rigorous derivation of the homogenization limit and the quantification of the convergence to the effective problem. In particular, we are interested in the case in which the holes in the domain under consideration are randomly distributed and may give rise to complicated geometries by overlapping and concentrating in certain regions of the domain.

      11/12/2020     Gero Friesecke (Technische Universität München)
      00/00/0000     High-dimensional quantum wavefunctions, optimal transport, and tensor networks
      00/00/0000    
PDEs from many-body physics (be it the kinetic Fokker-Planck equation governing molecular dynamics or the electronic Schr"odinger equation governing electronic structure) suffer from the curse of dimension. If the single-particle state space is discretized by L gridponts or basis functions, optimal (or candidate) solutions require L^N gridpoint values or coefficients.
Many semi-empirical schemes are being tried, often centered around some clever ansatz to parametrize high-dimensional functions. In this talk I pursue the following fundamental but poorly understood question: what, if anything, is known about the possiblity of really breaking the curse of dimension in such systems?
I present
- rigorous work with Daniela Voegler (SIAM J. Math. Anal. 50 (4), 3996-4019, 2018) where we discovered a low-dimensional ansatz manifold for the strictly correlated limit of the electronic Schroedinger equation. The limit is a high-dimensional optimal transport problem and the manifold contains the exact minimizers and breaks the curse.
- first results with Mi-Song Dupuy (SIAM J. Sci. Comp., to appear, 2020) which indicate that sparse tensor factorizations via tensor trains or tensor networks may not be enough unless combined with systematic optimization (''learning'') of the network.
